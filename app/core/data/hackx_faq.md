# HackX 11.0 Official FAQ


## General Information

**What is hackX?**
hackX is Sri Lanka's premier inter-university startup challenge that helps undergraduate students turn real-world problems into innovative solutions with startup potential.

**Who organizes hackX?**
hackX is organized by the Industrial Management Science Students' Association of the Department of Industrial Management, University of Kelaniya, in collaboration with the Ministry of Science and Technology and the National Science Foundation (NSF).

**What is the objective of hackX?**
The objective of hackX is to encourage innovation, entrepreneurship, and problem-solving among undergraduates by helping them develop startup ideas that address real-world challenges.

**Is hackX a hackathon?**
hackX is more than a traditional hackathon. It is a startup challenge that guides participants through multiple phases to develop, validate, and refine their ideas.

**Why should I participate in hackX?**
hackX provides mentorship, industry exposure, networking opportunities, startup experience, and the chance to win exciting prizes. Participants also gain access to valuable connections within Sri Lanka's startup ecosystem, including industry experts, government stakeholders, startup enablers, and angel investors. For promising teams, hackX can serve as a gateway to further support, funding opportunities, and programs that help transform innovative ideas into real startups.

**What makes hackX different from other competitions?**
hackX focuses on building startup-ready solutions rather than just developing prototypes or technical projects.

## Eligibility

**Who can participate in hackX?**
Undergraduate students from recognized universities and higher education institutions in Sri Lanka.

**Can students from any field of study participate?**
Yes. Students from all fields of study are welcome and encouraged to participate.

**Can students from any year of study participate?**
Yes. Undergraduates from first-year to final-year enrolled in any recognized university or higher education institute can participate.

**Can recent graduates participate?**
No. The competition is only open to undergraduates.

**How many members can be in a team?**
A team can consist of 1–5 members.

**Can I participate alone or as a team of two?**
Yes. Individual participants and teams of two are allowed. However, we recommend having at least three members in a team, as it makes it easier to share responsibilities and develop your idea more effectively. If your workload is manageable, participating alone or as a team of two is perfectly acceptable.

**Can team members be from different faculties?**
Yes. Team members can be from different faculties, as long as they represent the same university.

**Can team members be from different universities?**
No. All team members must be undergraduates representing the same university.

**How many teams can represent one university?**
There is no limit. Any number of teams can represent one university.

## Registration

**How do I register?**
Teams can register through the registration portal in the official website (hackx.lk).

**Is registration free?**
Yes. Participation is completely free, and no fee is charged from competitors at any stage of the competition.

**When does registration open?**
23rd June 2026

**Can I edit my registration details after submission?**
Yes, within the allowed period. Please contact the Organizing Committee for further details.

**What happens after registration?**
Teams will receive further instructions regarding proposal submission, workshops, and other upcoming phases.

**Will I receive a confirmation after registering?**
Yes. An email notification will be sent to the team leader, confirming your registration.

## Team Formation

**How do I find teammates?**
Students can form teams with friends, classmates, or other interested undergraduates within the same university.

**Can I change team members after registration?**
Any changes to registration details can only be made within the registration period.

**Can one student join multiple teams?**
One student cannot join multiple teams. If a student is found to be a member of more than one team, all teams associated with that student will be immediately disqualified.

**Can a team have members from different academic backgrounds?**
Yes. Teams with members from different faculties, degree programs, or skill sets are encouraged. Having a diverse team can bring different perspectives and strengths, helping you develop stronger and more innovative solutions.

## Idea Requirements

**What type of ideas can be submitted?**
You can submit any innovative solution that addresses a real-world problem and has the potential to become a startup. The solution can be a product, service, platform, mobile application, hardware device, or any other idea that creates value for users.

**Does my idea need to be technology-based?**
Not necessarily. Your idea does not have to be entirely technology-based, but most modern startups use some form of technology to solve problems more effectively. While it is not a requirement, including a technological component can help make your solution more innovative, scalable, and impactful.

**Can I submit a mobile app idea?**
Yes. Mobile applications are welcome as long as they solve a clearly identified problem and demonstrate value to users.

**Can I submit a hardware product idea?**
Yes. Product-based innovations, devices, and hardware solutions are accepted.

**Can I submit a service-based startup idea?**
Yes. Many successful startups are built around services rather than physical products. As long as your idea addresses a genuine need and has growth potential, it can be submitted.

**Does the idea need to solve a Sri Lankan problem?**
No. Your idea can address local, regional, or global challenges.

**Can I submit an idea that is already in the market?**
Ideas submitted must be original and developed by the participating team. However, it is acceptable if similar products, services, or competitors already exist in the market. In such cases, teams must clearly demonstrate what makes their solution unique, such as a distinctive feature, improved approach, better user experience, or a stronger value proposition.

**Can I submit an idea I have used in another competition?**
Yes. You may submit an idea that has been used in another competition, provided it does not violate any rules, eligibility requirements, or originality guidelines of either competition. Participants should carefully review the official hackX rules and guidelines to ensure their submission complies with all applicable requirements.

**Do I need a working prototype?**
During the initial stages, a strong understanding of the problem and a well-thought-out solution are more important than having a working prototype. However, teams advancing to the semi-finals and beyond will be expected to demonstrate a fully or partially developed prototype to showcase their solution and validate their concept.

## Proposal Submission

**What is a proposal?**
A proposal is a document that explains your startup idea. It should clearly describe the problem you are solving, who experiences the problem, how your solution works, why it is innovative, and how it could become a sustainable business. An official proposal template will be provided through the hackX website to help participants prepare their submissions according to the required format.

**How do I submit my proposal?**
Proposals must be submitted through the submission portal available on the official hackX website before the stated deadline.

**What should be included in the proposal?**
Please refer to the official proposal template and submission guidelines provided by the organizing committee. The template outlines all required sections and formatting requirements that must be followed when preparing your proposal.

**Can I edit my proposal after submission?**
Yes, but only until the submission deadline. If you resubmit your proposal before the deadline, the new submission will automatically replace and delete the previous submission. Once the deadline has passed, no further changes can be made.

**What happens if I miss the deadline?**
Late submissions will not be accepted. Teams are advised to submit their proposals well before the deadline to avoid any last-minute technical issues or submission delays.

**Is a pitching video required with the proposal?**
Yes. In addition to the proposal document, all teams must submit a pitching video as part of their proposal submission. Both the proposal and the pitching video are compulsory requirements.

**How long should the pitching video be?**
The pitching video should be between 2 and 3 minutes in length. Teams are advised to stay within this time limit and communicate their idea clearly and concisely.

**How do I submit the pitching video?**
Teams must upload their pitching video to YouTube as an Unlisted video and include the video link in the proposal submission portal. The video should not be submitted through any other platform unless otherwise specified by the organizers.

**What does "Unlisted" mean?**
An Unlisted YouTube video cannot be found through search results or on your public channel. Only people with the video link can view it. This allows judges and organizers to access your video while keeping it private from the public.

**What should be included in the pitching video?**
The pitching video should introduce your idea and explain the key aspects of your proposed solution. Detailed guidelines on the areas that should be covered will be provided in the Delegate Booklet.

**Can we be creative with the pitching video?**
Absolutely. Teams are encouraged to be creative in how they present their ideas. You may use presentations, demonstrations, animations, storytelling, interviews, prototypes, or any other format that effectively communicates your solution.

**Do all team members need to appear in the video?**
No. There is no requirement for all team members to appear on screen. Teams may decide how they wish to present their idea, provided the video clearly communicates the proposed solution.

**Can we use slides or screen recordings in our video?**
Yes. Teams may use slides, screen recordings, animations, product mockups, demonstrations, or any other visual aids that help explain their idea effectively.

**What happens if I submit only the proposal or only the video?**
Submissions are considered complete only when both the proposal document and the pitching video link have been submitted. Failure to submit either component may result in the submission being deemed incomplete.

**Can I edit or replace my pitching video after submission?**
Yes, but only until the proposal submission deadline. Any updates must be made before the deadline, after which no further changes will be accepted.

**Do I need professional video editing skills to create the pitching video?**
No. The purpose of the pitching video is to help judges understand your idea. Clear communication and a strong explanation of your solution are more important than professional production quality.

**Will the pitching video be evaluated?**
Yes. The pitching video forms part of the proposal submission and may be used by evaluators to better understand your problem, solution, innovation, and startup potential.

**What if my YouTube link is not accessible?**
Teams are responsible for ensuring that their video link works correctly and that the video's visibility is set to Unlisted. Inaccessible videos may affect the evaluation of the submission.

**Why is a pitching video required?**
The pitching video allows teams to communicate their ideas in a more engaging and effective way than a document alone. It also helps participants develop pitching and presentation skills that are essential in entrepreneurship and startup development.

## Competition Phases

**What are the phases of hackX?**
hackX is conducted through multiple phases designed to help participants gradually develop their ideas. These phases typically include registrations, proposal submissions, evaluations, workshops, semi-finals, mentoring programs,  and the grand finals.

**What happens during the semi-finals?**
Teams selected from the proposal evaluation stage will present their ideas to a panel of judges. This stage focuses on assessing the problem, solution, innovation, and startup potential of each concept.

**What happens during the grand finals?**
Finalist teams will showcase their refined ideas after receiving feedback and mentorship. They will present their solutions, business models, and future plans to a panel of industry experts, entrepreneurs, and academics.

**Will there be mentoring sessions?**
Yes. Finalists will have access to mentorship sessions designed to help strengthen their ideas, improve their presentations, and better understand startup development.

**Are workshops mandatory?**
While attendance requirements may vary, participants are strongly encouraged to attend all workshops and mentoring sessions, as they provide valuable knowledge and guidance throughout the competition.

## Judging & Evaluation

**How are proposals evaluated?**
Submissions are evaluated using several criteria, including innovation, feasibility, impact, market potential, scalability, business viability, and the overall quality of the presentation.

**Who evaluates the submissions?**
Submissions will be evaluated by experts and professionals from hackX's Knowledge Partner organizations. These evaluators bring relevant industry and domain expertise to assess the innovation, feasibility, impact, and startup potential of each submission.

**What are judges looking for?**
Judges typically look for teams that clearly understand the problem they are solving, offer a unique and practical solution, demonstrate market potential, and show the ability to turn the idea into a sustainable venture.

**Will feedback be provided?**
Feedback will be provided to semi-finalists and finalists during the pitching stages. Participants are encouraged to use this feedback to refine their ideas, strengthen their presentations, and further develop their startup concepts.

## Startup Development

**Do I need business knowledge to participate?**
No. hackX is designed for students from all academic backgrounds. Whether you study engineering, management, science, medicine, IT, arts, or any other field, you can participate. While prior business knowledge is not required, having some understanding of business concepts can be an added advantage when developing and presenting your startup idea.

**Will hackX help me learn entrepreneurship?**
Yes. One of the key goals of hackX is to introduce participants to entrepreneurship, startup thinking, innovation processes, and business development through practical experience.

**Will I receive mentorship?**
Yes. Finalists will have opportunities to interact with mentors who can provide guidance on innovation, business strategy, pitching, validation, and startup growth.

**Can hackX help me continue my startup after the competition?**
hackX aims to provide participants with the knowledge, exposure, and connections needed to continue developing their ideas beyond the competition. Throughout the semi-final and final rounds, teams will present their ideas to a diverse panel of judges that may include industry specialists, academics, government representatives, entrepreneurs, and investors. This provides valuable opportunities for participants to receive expert feedback, build professional networks, and potentially attract support for further developing their startups. Many teams use hackX as a starting point for their entrepreneurial journey.

**Can I seek investors through hackX?**
While investment is not guaranteed, hackX provides exposure to Sri Lanka's startup ecosystem, including industry professionals, startup enablers, government stakeholders, and potential investors. As a national-level startup challenge, it offers promising teams valuable opportunities to gain visibility, build connections, and explore future funding and growth opportunities.

## Awards & Benefits

The top-performing teams will compete for the following cash prizes:

2nd Place: LKR 150,000
3rd Place: LKR 125,000
In addition to cash prizes, participants gain valuable mentorship, industry exposure, networking opportunities, startup experience, and potential connections to investors and ecosystem partners.

**Are there certificates?**
Yes. Certificates will be awarded to teams that qualify for the Semi-Finals and Grand Finals.

**Do all participants receive certificates?**
Teams that progress to the Semi-Finals and Grand Finals will receive certificates recognizing their achievement, commitment, and success in advancing through the competition.

Yes. hackX 11.0 features a Most Popular Team Award, which carries a cash prize of LKR 30,000.
Additional awards, prizes, or special recognition categories may also be introduced through sponsor and partner collaborations. Any such awards will be announced through the official hackX channels during the competition.

## Intellectual Property

**Who owns the idea I submit?**
Participants retain ownership of their ideas and intellectual property. hackX does not claim ownership of solutions submitted to the competition.

**Will my idea be shared publicly?**
Your idea may be viewed by judges, mentors, and authorized organizers as part of the evaluation process. Any public sharing will be conducted in accordance with competition policies.

**How is my information protected?**
Participant information is handled responsibly and used only for competition-related purposes in accordance with applicable policies and guidelines.

## Ambassador Program

**What is the hackX Ambassador Network?**
The hackX Ambassador Network is a community of student representatives from universities across Sri Lanka who help promote hackX within their campuses and encourage student participation in innovation and entrepreneurship.

**Who is a hackX Ambassador?**
A hackX Ambassador is the official representative of hackX at their university. Ambassadors act as a bridge between the hackX organizing committee and their campus community.

**Who can apply to become a hackX Ambassador?**
Any enthusiastic undergraduate student can apply. Batch representatives, society leaders, club members, and other active students with strong campus networks are especially encouraged to apply.

**What are the responsibilities of a hackX Ambassador?**
Ambassadors help create awareness about hackX, share updates and promotional materials, encourage student participation, and connect the organizing committee with relevant student societies, clubs, and communities.

**Do I need previous experience to become an Ambassador?**
No. Previous experience is not required. We are looking for motivated students who are interested in leadership, innovation, entrepreneurship, and community engagement.

**Can I be both a hackX Ambassador and a hackX participant?**
Yes. Students may apply as Ambassadors while also participating in hackX, subject to any competition policies and guidelines.

**What are the benefits of becoming a hackX Ambassador?**
Ambassadors receive official recognition from hackX, an official hackX Ambassador Certificate, access to exclusive workshops, networking opportunities, and the chance to connect with students, professionals, and industry leaders.

**Will Ambassadors receive a certificate?**
Yes. Ambassadors who successfully fulfill their responsibilities will receive an official hackX Ambassador Certificate.

**Are there awards for Ambassadors?**
Yes. Outstanding Ambassadors may receive special recognition and be featured as top-performing members of the hackX Ambassador Network.

**How much time does being an Ambassador require?**
The commitment is designed to be manageable alongside academic work. Responsibilities mainly involve sharing updates, promoting hackX activities, and engaging with students within your university.

**How many Ambassadors will be selected from each university?**
There is no fixed limit on the number of Ambassadors from a university. Any eligible student who registers for the hackX Ambassador Network can become an Ambassador and contribute to promoting hackX within their campus. We encourage active students from all faculties, societies, and batches to get involved.

**Will I receive training as an Ambassador?**
Yes. Ambassadors will receive guidance, resources, and support from the hackX organizing committee to help them carry out their role effectively.

**What skills can I gain as a hackX Ambassador?**
Ambassadors can develop leadership, communication, networking, event promotion, teamwork, and community-building skills while working closely with a national-level innovation initiative.

**Why should I become a hackX Ambassador?**
The hackX Ambassador Program is a unique opportunity to represent a national startup challenge, expand your professional network, develop valuable leadership skills, gain official recognition, and contribute to building a stronger culture of innovation and entrepreneurship within your university.

**How can I apply to become a hackX Ambassador?**
Applications can be submitted through the official hackX Ambassador registration portal. Details and deadlines will be announced through the official hackX channels.

**What makes a strong Ambassador candidate?**
Students who are active within their university, have good communication skills, are well-connected with student communities, and are passionate about innovation and entrepreneurship are likely to be strong candidates for the program.

**Will being a hackX Ambassador help my career?**
The experience can strengthen your leadership profile, expand your professional network, and demonstrate initiative and community engagement, which can be valuable for future academic and career opportunities.

**How do Ambassadors earn points?**
Ambassadors earn points through various activities within the hackX Ambassador Program. This includes getting teams to register for hackX using their unique Ambassador Code, conducting awareness activities, and supporting participant engagement throughout the competition.

**What is an Ambassador Code?**
Each Ambassador receives a unique Ambassador Code upon registration. Teams can enter this code during hackX registration, allowing the Ambassador to receive points for referring that team to the competition.

**Do I earn points when a team registers using my Ambassador Code?**
Yes. Ambassadors receive points for each eligible team that registers using their Ambassador Code during the competition registration process.

**Can I earn points after a team registers?**
Yes. Ambassadors may earn additional points when teams registered through their Ambassador Code successfully progress to later stages of the competition, such as the Semi-Finals and Grand Finals.

**Can I earn points through awareness activities?**
Yes. Ambassadors can earn points by conducting awareness and promotional activities within their universities. Evidence of these activities can be uploaded through the Ambassador Dashboard for verification.

**How do I submit proof of awareness activities?**
Ambassadors can upload photos, screenshots, event details, or other supporting materials through the Ambassador Dashboard. These submissions will be reviewed before points are awarded.

**How are Ambassador awards decided?**
Ambassador awards and recognitions are based on the total points earned throughout the program. The more impact an Ambassador creates, the higher their score will be.

**Can I track my points?**
Yes. Ambassadors can view their points, activity records, and overall performance through the Ambassador Dashboard.

**Is there a leaderboard for Ambassadors?**
Yes. The Ambassador Dashboard includes a leaderboard that allows Ambassadors to track their ranking and compare their progress with other Ambassadors across the country.

**How can I improve my position on the leaderboard?**
You can increase your score by encouraging more teams to register using your Ambassador Code, supporting teams throughout the competition, and actively conducting and reporting awareness activities.

**Are the leaderboard rankings updated regularly?**
Yes. Rankings will be updated as points are verified and added to the system throughout the competition period.

**What rewards can top-performing Ambassadors receive?**
Top-performing Ambassadors will receive special recognition and may be eligible for additional awards, opportunities, and benefits announced by the organizing committee throughout the program.

**Can I see which teams used my Ambassador Code?**
Yes. Ambassadors will be able to view relevant referral and performance information through their Ambassador Dashboard. Subject to privacy policies, this may include the number of teams registered through their code and their progress in the competition.
