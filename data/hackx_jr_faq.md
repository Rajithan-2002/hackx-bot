# HackX Jr. 9.0 Official FAQ

## General Questions
**What is hackX Jr.?**
hackX Jr. is an inter-school innovation competition organized by the Industrial Management Science Students' Association (IMSSA) of the University of Kelaniya. It provides a platform for school students across Sri Lanka to showcase their creativity, problem-solving abilities, and innovative thinking by developing practical solutions to real-world challenges.

**Why is hackX Jr. recognized as a leading innovation competition?**
hackX Jr. is currently in its 9th edition, reflecting a strong legacy of fostering innovation among school students. This year's programme is conducted in collaboration with the Ministry of Science and Technology and the National Science Foundation (NSF) for the second consecutive year and is approved by the Ministry of Education. These partnerships strengthen the competition's national recognition and impact.

**What are the objectives of hackX Jr. 9.0?**
Guided by the theme "Give Shapes to Ideas", hackX Jr. 9.0 aims to encourage students to turn innovative ideas into practical solutions for real-world challenges while developing their creativity, critical thinking, and problem-solving skills.

**Is hackX Jr. 9.0 a national-level competition?**
Yes. It is a prestigious national-level competition open to talented school students from all across Sri Lanka.

**What does the term “hackathon” mean in the context of hackX Jr.?**
In the context of hackX Jr., a hackathon is an innovation competition where students identify real-world problems and develop creative, practical, and impactful solutions within a given timeframe. While technology can be used as a tool, the primary focus is on innovation, problem-solving, and turning ideas into reality.

**What opportunities does hackX Jr. 9.0 provide for students?**
hackX Jr. 9.0 provides students with the opportunity to develop their innovation, creativity, and problem-solving skills while turning their ideas into practical solutions. Participants can pitch their ideas to industry experts, gain valuable knowledge through free online sessions, showcase their talents on a national platform, and compete for attractive cash prizes and rewards.

## Eligibility & Team Formation
**Who can participate in hackX Jr.?**
The competition is open to all school students across Sri Lanka from Grade 8 up to the G.C.E. Advanced Level (2026 A/L batch). If you are in this range, you are fully eligible to join and showcase your innovation.

**How many members can be in a team?**
Your team can have anywhere from 1 to 5 members. But To ensure fair play, all members of your team must belong to your own school.

**Can I participate alone?**
Absolutely! Solo innovators are completely welcome. If you have a brilliant idea and want to take on the challenge by yourself, you can register as a one member team.

**Can students from different schools be in one team?**
No, they cannot. To ensure fair play, all members of your team must belong to your own school. Combining forces with friends from other schools is strictly against the rules, and doing so will result in disqualification.

**Can I join more than one team?**
No, you must choose only one team. A student can only participate in a single team throughout the hackathon. Please be careful: if a student's name appears on two different team registries, both teams will be automatically disqualified without exception.

**Can our school register multiple teams?**
Yes, definitely! There is no limit on how many teams a single school can send. Schools are highly encouraged to submit multiple teams to showcase different creative minds and maximize their chances of winning.

**Do we need a teacher-in-charge?**
It is completely optional. You are not legally required to have a teacher to register your team. However, assigning a teacher-in-charge is highly encouraged because having a mentor can give you great guidance, moral support, and coordination help throughout your journey.

## Registrations
**How do I register my team?**
You can easily register online by visiting our official website at [jr.hackx.lk](https://jr.hackx.lk). Just head over to the registration page during the open registration period and fill out the online form.

**Is registration free?**
Yes. Participation is completely free, and no fee is charged from competitors at any stage of the competition.

**When does registration open?**
Registrations officially open on 23rd June 2026.

**What is the registration deadline?**
Make sure to lock in your spot early! The registration window will close on 28th July 2026. Late entries will not be accepted, so keep an eye on the date.

**What information is needed for registration?**
To complete your registration, you will need to accurately provide the following details:
- The Team Leader's contact details
- Your official School Name
- The full names and information of all team members
- Optional but Highly Recommended: The name and details of your Teacher-in-Charge to ensure your team has formal backing and support.

**Does every team member need to register?**
No, you don't all need to sign up separately. Registration by a single team member (such as the team leader) is completely sufficient for the whole group. Just ensure that whoever fills out the form on [jr.hackx.lk](https://jr.hackx.lk) has the correct and complete details for everyone on the team.

**Can I edit my registration after submitting it?**
The portal does not allow you to change details on your own once submitted. If you made a mistake or need to update your information, please reach out directly to the organizing committee for assistance, and they will help you fix it. But, within the allowed period.

**What happens after registration?**
Teams will receive further instructions regarding proposal submission, workshops, and other upcoming phases.

**Will I receive a confirmation after registering?**
Yes. An email notification will be sent to the team leader, confirming your registration.

**Can I change team members after registration?**
Any changes to registration details can only be made within the registration period.

## Ideas
**What kind of idea can I submit?**
Any idea that brings a fresh approach to a real problem. Your solution must be original, creative, novel, and practical. It should reflect your team's independent thinking and focus on resolving a genuine, real-world issue effectively.

**What domains or areas can our idea focus on?**
You have complete creative freedom! Your project can target any domain that needs innovation. Popular areas include:
- Education & Technology (EdTech)
- Agriculture & Environment (e.g., sustainable farming, climate tech)
- Healthcare & Wellbeing
- Tourism & Transportation
- Community Development or any other field where technology can drive positive change.

**Can you suggest some project ideas?**
If you are looking for inspiration, here are a few great examples of the types of projects you could build:
- Smart Irrigation Controller (automating water conservation for farmers)
- School Bus Tracking App (improving safety and logistics for students and parents)
- Smart Waste Segregation Bin (using automation or sorting to encourage eco-friendly recycling)
- Medicine Reminder Device (helping elderly or chronic patients stay on top of their health)

**Does my idea need to solve a real-world problem?**
Yes, absolutely. The core goal of hackX Jr. 9.0 is to tackle practical challenges. Theoretical concepts that do not offer a clear, tangible solution to a real-world problem will not score well with the judging panel.

**Can we submit more than one idea?**
Yes, you can throw multiple ideas into the mix. Each team is welcome to submit more than one concept during the initial phase. However, please note that only one selected idea per team will be chosen to move forward into the actual rounds of the competition.

**What makes a good hackX Jr. idea?**
A winning idea beautifully balances four key pillars:
- Innovation: Is it unique, or does it improve an existing solution in a clever way?
- Practicality & Impact: Does it genuinely make life better or solve a painful problem?
- Feasibility: Can it actually be built and implemented with current technology, or is it too unrealistic?

**Can our idea be a mobile app?**
Yes. Mobile apps are a fantastic way to solve problems, whether they are built for iOS, Android, or cross-platform use.

**Can our idea be a website?**
Absolutely. Web applications, platforms, and digital portals that connect users or solve logistical issues are fully accepted.

**Can our idea be hardware-based?**
Yes, hardware projects are completely welcome. You can build physical devices using microcontrollers like Arduino, Raspberry Pi, or any IoT (Internet of Things) tech to interact with the physical world.

**Can we use AI in our solution?**
Yes, definitely. Integrating Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, Computer Vision, or data analytics is highly encouraged if it genuinely enhances the smart capabilities of your solution.

**Does my idea need to be entirely technology-based?**
Not necessarily. For hackX Jr. 9.0, your idea does not have to be entirely tech-based, but most modern solutions use some form of technology to solve problems effectively. While it is not a strict requirement for initial concepts, including a technological component is highly encouraged as it helps make your solution more innovative, scalable, and impactful for the competition.

**Can I submit an idea I have used in another competition?**
Yes, you can! You may submit an idea used in another competition, provided it does not violate any rules, eligibility requirements, or originality guidelines of either event. Just ensure your submission fully complies with all official hackX Jr. 9.0 guidelines and reflects your team's own thinking.

**Do I need a working prototype?**
Not at the beginning. During the initial stages and the Semi-Finals, a strong understanding of the problem and a well-thought-out idea are what matter most prototypes are not required at the Semi-Final stage. However, teams advancing to the Grand Finals of hackX Jr. 9.0 will be paired with mentors and expected to demonstrate a prototype to validate their concept.

**How innovative does the idea need to be?**
It doesn't have to be a brand-new invention that changes the entire world overnight. Innovation can simply mean taking a problem that already has a solution and making it significantly cheaper, faster, more accessible, or uniquely tailored to a specific audience (like a local issue in Sri Lanka).

## Proposal Submission
**What is a proposal?**
A proposal is a formal document that explains your project or startup idea for hackX Jr. 9.0. It clearly describes the problem you are solving, your target audience, how your solution works, why it is innovative, and how it could become sustainable. The official proposal template will be provided on our website to guide you through the required format.

**When can we submit our proposal, and what is the deadline?**
The proposal submission window opens on 31st July 2026 and closes on 14th August 2026. Please make sure to submit before the deadline, as late or partially completed submissions will be rejected.

**What should be included in the proposal?**
Your proposal must strictly follow the official template and guidelines provided by the organizing committee on the website. The template outlines all the required sections, layouts, and the 3,500-word limit.

**Can I edit my proposal after submission?**
Yes, but only until the final deadline on 14th August 2026. If you upload a revised proposal before the deadline, the new PDF will automatically replace your previous submission. No changes can be made once the deadline passes.

**What happens if I miss the deadline?**
Late or incomplete submissions will not be accepted under any circumstances. We highly advise teams to upload their proposals well before the deadline to avoid last-minute technical issues or internet delays.

**In what format and language should the proposal be submitted?**
The proposal must be uploaded as a PDF document and must be written in English only.

**Is there a word limit, and can we include visuals?**
Yes, the maximum word limit is 3,500 words. You are highly encouraged to include diagrams, images, charts, and other relevant visuals to help explain your idea clearly.

**Where can I find the proposal template, and can I see it beforehand?**
The official proposal template will be made available on our official website ([jr.hackx.lk](https://jr.hackx.lk)) right when the proposal submission period begins.

**Can I resubmit or make changes to my proposal after uploading?**
To modify a submission, you must carefully follow the specific instructions provided directly on the submission platform. Keep in mind that incomplete proposals will be automatically rejected, so ensure everything is finalized before uploading.

## Awareness Session
**What is the Awareness Session?**
It is an introductory session designed to guide school students on how to brainstorm creative ideas, write a winning project proposal, and understand the core concepts of innovation. It's the perfect starting point for your hackX Jr. 9.0 journey.

**When is the Awareness Session?**
The session will take place on 3rd July 2026.

**Where will the Awareness Session be held?**
It will be held physically at the Department of Industrial Management, University of Kelaniya.

**Is attending the Awareness Session mandatory?**
No, it is not mandatory. You do not need to attend to register or compete in the hackathon. In fact, even if you don't plan to compete, you are still welcome to join just to learn.
*(Note: If you reserve a physical seat, please make sure to attend. If you can't make it, please leave that spot for another student who really needs it!)*

**Can we join the Awareness Session online?**
Yes. For students who cannot travel to the university, the session will be broadcast online as well. The joining links will be shared on our official platforms ahead of time.

**What will I learn during the Awareness Session?**
You will gain essential skills directly from university lecturers and industry experts, including:
- **Idea Generation**: How to spot real-world problems and think of smart solutions.
- **Proposal Writing**: Step-by-step instructions on how to fill out the official template.
- **Tools & Techniques**: An introduction to modern tech tools and industry insights that will make your idea stand out.

**Will the online broadcast allow us to ask questions?**
Yes. The online session will feature a live Q&A segment where online participants can type in their questions regarding registration, idea limits, or proposal guidelines, and the organizing team will answer them live.

**If I miss the live Awareness Session on 3rd July, can I still watch it later?**
Yes. A recording of the session will remain available online so that students who couldn't make it to the physical venue or missed the live stream can catch up and learn how to write a winning proposal before the submission portal opens on 31st July.

**What are the event timings for the Awareness Session?**
The Awareness Session will be held from 9:00 AM to 1:30 PM on 3rd July 2026. Please make sure to arrive early if you are attending physically at the University of Kelaniya, or log in on time if you are joining us online, so you don't miss any of the expert guidance.
